The rest of the episode just made people feel a bit drunk.</p><p></p><p><strong>Actor Shia LaBeouf </strong><a href=\"http://www.vulture.com/2013/12/shia-labeouf-plagiarizes-more-apologies.html\" title=\"\"><strong>plagiarised a series of apologies</strong></a><strong> for plagiarising dialogue in his short film from a graphic novel.</strong> File this one under: \"pretentious misdirection\" and \"aggressive contrition\".</p><p></p><p><strong>The Christmas No 1 was a song </strong><a href=\"http://www.lyricsontop.com/sam-bailey-songs/skyscraper-lyrics.html\" title=\"\"><strong>about urban renewal from the point of view of a building</strong></a><strong>.</strong> Fans of The X Factor winner Sam Bailey's Skyscraper can bandy the word \"metaphor\" about all they like. The rest of us know the truth.</p><p></p><p><strong>Vogue editor-in-chief Anna Wintour threw out her family's Christmas tree before Christmas Day because it looked \"too messy\". </strong>Her daughter <a href=\"http://instagram.com/p/iWo1QGAMfS/\" title=\"\">posted a&nbsp;picture to Instagram</a> of their presents piled against a wall. It looked as festive as a grave.</p><p></p><p><strong>Sir Alex Ferguson turned up as the new bad guy in Sherlock.</strong> We only glimpsed the villain's eyes, but the resemblance was so striking that hundreds of viewers took to Twitter and message boards to see if anyone thought the same. They did. We all did.</p><p></p><p><strong>Edward Snowden told us a child born today would never have an \"unrecorded, unanalysed thought\".</strong> The whistleblower made the claim in his alternative to the Queen's speech, <a href=\"http://www.channel4.com/news/edward-snowden-nsa-gchq-whistleblower-surveillance-spying\" title=\"\">broadcast on Channel 4 on Christmas Day</a>.
